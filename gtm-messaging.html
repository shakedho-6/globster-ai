Brand Voice

Language Rules

Language is product. The words we choose signal who we are and who we're for. Consistency here is not cosmetic — it's strategic.

Words We Use
Agent kit / Kit
Concrete, ownable, role-specific. "Kit" implies completeness — everything is already in the box. Differentiates from generic "agents" or "tools."
Deploy
Implies speed and readiness. "Deploy in one click" signals that the hard work is done. Contrast with "build" or "set up."
Pre-configured / Already knows your job
The core differentiator in plain language. Signals that our agents come with context, not blank state.
Results / Outcomes
Buyers care about outputs, not processes. Lead with what gets done, not how the agent works.
Delegate / Done
Emotionally resonant for overwhelmed buyers. The fantasy is delegation, not configuration. "Just approve" and "it's already done" speak to the feeling.
Your team / Team of agents
Positions Globster as a staffing solution, not a software subscription. Elevates the value perception from "tool" to "capability."
Words We Avoid
AI platform / AI solution
Generic category language. Every tool in our space uses this. It says nothing distinctive and triggers "another AI thing" fatigue.
Build / Create / Configure
Implies work for the user. Our entire positioning is that the work is already done. Using "build" contradicts the promise.
Workflow automation
Sounds like Zapier. Cold, mechanical. We're not automating workflows — we're delegating outcomes to a role-aware agent. Different category.
Cutting-edge / State-of-the-art
Empty adjectives that every tech company uses. Our ICP doesn't care about the model. They care about whether it does their job.
Prompt / Prompting
This word signals effort and expertise. "You need to know how to prompt it" is the exact fear we're alleviating. Don't surface it.
Scalable / Enterprise-grade
Enterprise signals to our SMB ICP that the product isn't for them — too complex, too expensive. We can be robust without claiming enterprise.
