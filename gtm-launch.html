GTM 2026 · Launch Sequence

Launch Sequence

We don't announce, then go quiet. We build in public, ship in phases, and treat every milestone as a distribution event.

Section 01

The Four Phases

Each phase has a single goal. We don't move to the next phase until the current one's go-criteria are met. This is how we avoid scaling before we're ready.

Now → Jun 15, 2026
Phase 0 — Foundation
"Get the infrastructure right before we invite anyone in."
  • Ship the Marketing Kit v1: content brief agent, campaign calendar agent, competitor monitor agent, email sequence agent. All 1-click deploy, no configuration required.
  • Onboard 5 beta users through founder direct outreach. Goal is learning, not growth — use weekly 30-min check-ins to map the real activation journey.
  • Establish LinkedIn posting cadence. First 6 weeks are for finding which pillar and hook style resonates with target ICP. Track impressions and engagement rate per post.
  • Define and instrument the core events in the product: signup, kit deploy, first task run, team invite sent, D7 active, D30 active. Analytics in place before Phase 1 starts.
Jun 15 → Aug 31, 2026
Phase 1 — Marketing Lane
"Prove the marketing kit works well enough that users tell colleagues about it."
  • Public launch of the Marketing Kit. LinkedIn post announcing it goes out Jun 15 with a before/after demo video. First-comment link to signup.
  • Target 20+ new signups/week by Week 4. Track activation rate (kit deploy within 24h of signup). Must exceed 60% activation before scaling any channel.
  • First 3 case studies published by Aug 1 — one per "result" type: time saved, campaign velocity, team handoff eliminated. These feed the content flywheel.
  • Referral mechanics live by Jun 22: in-product "invite your team" prompt, D7 referral nudge, and "Made with Globster" agent output attribution. Measure organic referral % weekly.
Sep → Oct, 2026
Phase 2 — Expand
"Add two more job-role kits and prove the model isn't just a marketing fluke."
  • Launch Sales Kit and HR Kit based on ICP interviews from Phase 1. Each kit must have at least 3 reference customers before public launch.
  • Begin LinkedIn retargeting campaign targeting Phase 1 site visitors with $500/month budget. A/B test two ad creatives against each other. Measure CAC vs organic cohort.
  • First team-tier pricing introduced. Goal: 5 teams (3+ users) converted from individual accounts by Oct 31. WAA metric should be higher in team accounts — validate this.
  • Community strategy: regular presence in 3 Slack communities (Demand Curve, Growthmentor, RevOps Co-op). Measure referral traffic and signups per community per week.
Nov → Dec, 2026
Phase 3 — PMF Sprint
"Hit the three PMF criteria simultaneously and declare product-market fit."
  • D30 retention target: >25% of users who signed up 30 days ago are still running at least one agent per week. If below 25%, hold and iterate on onboarding — do not continue growth spend.
  • Run first NPS survey at D30 for all users who activated. Target NPS >50. Qualitative follow-up calls with all Detractors (score ≤6) to understand churn drivers.
  • Organic referral metric: >20% of new signups should come from referral sources (not LinkedIn, not paid). This validates word-of-mouth is working without our active involvement.
  • If all three PMF criteria met by Dec 15: prepare 2027 scale plan, begin fundraising conversations, expand kit library to 5 roles. Celebrate publicly on LinkedIn — this is a distribution event too.
Section 02

Phase 1 Launch Checklist

The items that need to be true before, during, and after launch day on Jun 15. Each owner is responsible for checking their items off before the next gate.

Pre-Launch (complete by Jun 13)
  • Marketing Kit is deployed and end-to-end tested by all 5 beta users. No critical bugs open.
  • Landing page live with ICP-specific headline, demo video, and 3 beta user quotes. Mobile-tested.
  • Analytics instrumented: signup, kit deploy, first task run, D7 active events all firing correctly in production.
  • LinkedIn launch post drafted, reviewed, and scheduled for Jun 15 at 8am (peak feed time). Demo video exported and uploaded natively.
  • Referral mechanics live in product: "invite team" prompt, D7 nudge email, and "Made with Globster" output footer.
  • Onboarding email sequence set up: Day 0 (welcome + first task guide), Day 3 (tips based on kit type), Day 7 (referral nudge + case study).
  • Support inbox monitored — Slack channel or Intercom live with <2h response SLA for launch week.
  • Phase 1 KPI dashboard live and visible to team: signups/week, activation rate, D7 retention, WAA.
Launch Day (Jun 15)
  • LinkedIn post goes live at 8am. Reply to every comment within 1 hour. DM anyone who expresses direct interest.
  • Post in 3 communities (Reddit r/marketing, Demand Curve Slack, relevant HN thread if applicable). Authentic, non-spammy framing.
  • Monitor signup volume in real time. If server load spikes, have infrastructure plan ready (autoscaling enabled).
  • Personally onboard the first 5 signups with a 10-minute Loom video walkthrough sent within 1 hour of their signup.
  • End-of-day recap post: "We launched today. Here's what happened in the first 8 hours." (Builds in-public narrative.)
Week 1 (Jun 15–22)
  • Reach out personally to all users who signed up but didn't deploy a kit within 24h. One-sentence offer: "I'll set it up for you in 5 minutes."
  • Analyze Day 1 activation data: what % deployed a kit? What % ran their first task? Identify the drop-off point.
  • Publish a second LinkedIn post on Day 3: "We got X signups in our first 48 hours. Here's what surprised us."
  • First weekly 30-min team metrics review: signups, activation rate, any early D7 data available, qualitative user feedback.
  • Identify top 3 most engaged users and schedule 20-min user interviews for the following week to collect quote and case study material.
Month 1 (by Jul 15)
  • First case study published. Real numbers, real customer name (with permission), real before/after. Promoted as a LinkedIn post + sent to existing user base.
  • Activation rate stable at >60%. If not, run a 3-option onboarding A/B test: current flow vs. guided wizard vs. white-glove setup call offer.
  • D7 retention data available for first cohort. Target: >40%. If below, analyze which users retained and what they did differently in their first 3 days.
  • Organic referral % tracked. If >15%, referral mechanics are working — amplify. If <10%, audit the in-product share prompts and simplify the invite flow.
  • Month 1 retrospective: what did we learn, what changes for Month 2, what's the revised signup target for Phase 1 completion?
Section 03

Distribution Events

Every milestone is an announcement. We don't wait until we've "made it" to tell the story publicly — we tell it in real time. This builds an audience while building the product.

Milestone 01
First 10 Users
How we announce it: LinkedIn post — "10 heads of marketing are now running agents with Globster. Here's what they said in week 1." Include 2–3 direct quotes.

Expected reach: 2,000–6,000 impressions

Goal: Convert 5% of post viewers to signup (100–300 new leads in the pipeline).
Milestone 02
First Case Study
How we announce it: LinkedIn thread — "[Company] saves 6 hours/week. Here's the exact agent setup." Link to full case study page. Tag the customer (with permission).

Expected reach: 5,000–15,000 impressions (customer's network amplifies)

Goal: 3–5% conversion. This is the highest-converting content format we'll produce.
Milestone 03
First Team Deployment
How we announce it: LinkedIn post — "First full marketing team running on Globster. Not just one person — the whole team. Here's how they set it up." Product screenshot included.

Expected reach: 3,000–10,000 impressions

Goal: Drive team-tier signups. This post targets heads of marketing who want to go beyond individual use.
Milestone 04
First Referral Signup
How we announce it: Short LinkedIn post — "Today someone signed up for Globster because a colleague recommended it. We didn't ask them to. That's the whole game." Simple, authentic, no CTA needed.

Expected reach: 1,500–4,000 impressions

Goal: Signal product-market fit momentum publicly. Builds investor narrative too.
Milestone 05
PMF Signal Detected
How we announce it: Long-form LinkedIn post — "We hit the three metrics we set for product-market fit. D30 retention, NPS, organic referral. Here's what we learned building in public." Full breakdown.

Expected reach: 10,000–40,000 impressions (high share potential)

Goal: Massive top-of-funnel awareness + fundraising credibility signal.
Section 04

Go / No-Go Criteria

We advance phases on evidence, not optimism. These criteria are the gates. If we're not through them, we don't move forward — we fix what's broken first.

Transition Go Criteria No-Go Signal Decision Owner
Phase 0 → Phase 1 5 beta users completed full onboarding. Activation rate >60% in beta cohort. All core analytics events firing correctly. LinkedIn posting cadence established (at least 3 weeks of 4x/week). Marketing Kit has zero P0 bugs. Activation rate <40% in beta, OR beta users can't reach first value in <15 min, OR analytics infrastructure incomplete, OR fewer than 3 beta users giving positive qualitative feedback. Founder + Product lead. Unanimous decision required.
Phase 1 → Phase 2 ≥20 new signups/week for 3 consecutive weeks. Activation rate ≥60% sustained. D7 retention ≥35% in first cohort. At least 1 published case study. WAA trending toward 60%. Organic referral >10% of signups. Signup growth plateaued at <10/week, OR D7 retention <25% (retention problem must be fixed before expanding), OR activation rate <50%, OR no published case study, OR >30% of activated users churning before Day 14. Founder decision. No unanimous requirement — founder has final call.
Phase 2 → Phase 3 Sales Kit and HR Kit each have ≥3 reference customers. Team-tier has ≥5 paying teams. WAA ≥55% across all active users. CAC <$50 blended. Organic referral ≥15% sustained for 4 weeks. D30 retention ≥20% in Phase 1 cohort. Any new kit has activation rate <50% (stop and fix), OR team churn is higher than individual churn (pricing or value problem), OR organic referral has stalled <10% despite referral mechanics being live, OR D30 retention regressed below 15%. Founder + investor/advisor review. Requires external validation that PMF criteria are achievable in 60 days.
The No-Go Rule
A no-go decision is not a failure. It's the correct response to data. When we hit a no-go signal, we have a focused 2-week sprint to diagnose and fix the root cause. The two most common causes historically: (1) onboarding friction that prevents activation, and (2) kit value that doesn't match what users actually needed. Fix the onboarding or redefine the kit — don't add a new channel to mask the problem.
