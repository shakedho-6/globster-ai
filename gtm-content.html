GTM 2026 · Content Strategy

Content Strategy

Content isn't a marketing channel for us — it's the proof. Every post should make a head of marketing think "I need this" before they even click.

Section 01

Content Pillars

Four pillars. Each serves a different psychological job. Together they move someone from "never heard of Globster" to "when can I start?"

Pillar 1
The Gap
Job: build authority. Show we see what others don't.

Problems AI hasn't actually solved for marketing teams, counterintuitive observations, and honest takes on where AI tools fall short.

Example posts:
"Why your AI writing tool is still costing you 3 hours a day"
"The automation nobody is talking about for Q1 planning"
"I tested 6 AI marketing tools. Here's the one job none of them do"
Pillar 2
The Result
Job: build desire. Show the before and after.

Real customer stories with before/after metrics, founder experiments run with Globster agents, and case studies from actual deployments.

Example posts:
"Our head of marketing reclaimed 6 hours/week. Here's exactly how."
"Before Globster: 4 hours to brief. After: 12 minutes."
"I ran our entire content operation for 2 weeks using only AI agents. Results inside."
Pillar 3
The Method
Job: build trust. Give real value before asking for anything.

Step-by-step workflow walkthroughs, agent setup guides, and how-to frameworks that work regardless of whether you use Globster.

Example posts:
"How to set up a content brief agent in under 10 minutes (step by step)"
"The 5-agent marketing stack for teams under 10"
"My exact process for automating competitor monitoring with AI"
Pillar 4
The Opinion
Job: build reach. Controversy triggers the algorithm.

Provocative, defensible takes on where AI marketing is headed, what mainstream tools get wrong, and unpopular truths about the industry.

Example posts:
"Most 'AI for marketing' tools are just ChatGPT with a paywall. Change my mind."
"The AI marketing stack in 2026 will kill the coordinator role. Here's the evidence."
"Automation shouldn't require a prompt engineer. If it does, it's not ready."

Execution Rule
Never post without a hook in the first line. The first sentence determines whether LinkedIn shows the post to anyone. Write it last, after the body, when you know exactly what the payoff is. Good hook formula: Counterintuitive claim + implicit promise. Example: "Most AI marketing tools are actually creating more work. Here's why — and what to do instead."

Not all formats are equal. Text posts are our workhorse — they get produced in 45 minutes, not 4 hours. Everything else is an occasional amplifier.

Primary · 3–4× / week
Production time 45–90 min
Expected reach 500–5,000 / post
Conversion estimate 1–4% to signup
Why it works LinkedIn boosts text-native content. No competing tab to close.
Carousels

Section 04

Measurement

Three metrics tell us whether content is actually working — not vanity metrics like follower count, but leading indicators tied to business outcomes.

Metric 01 · Awareness
Engagement Rate >1.5%
Calculated as (likes + comments + shares) ÷ impressions. Below 1% means the content isn't resonating — rewrite the hook and test a different pillar. Above 3% is a winner to double down on with a follow-up post or carousel on the same topic.

Phase 0 baseline: 0.8% (establishing voice)
Phase 1 target: >1.5% average across all posts
Phase 2 target: >2% on Method + Result pillars
Metric 02 · Interest
Click-through Rate >2%
Of everyone who sees the post, how many click the link in the first comment or the profile link? Below 1% means the hook created curiosity but the body didn't earn the click. Rewrite the mid-post CTA. Above 4% is exceptional — analyze what the top performers have in common.

Phase 0 baseline: 0.5–1% (no established trust yet)
Phase 1 target: >2% on posts with explicit product CTAs
Phase 2 target: >3% driven by case study and demo content
Metric 03 · Revenue signal
Content → Signup >3%
Of everyone who clicks through from content, how many complete signup? Tracked via UTM source tags on all content links. Below 2% means the landing page isn't converting the right audience — check if LinkedIn audience matches ICP. Above 5% means content is qualifying well.

Phase 0 baseline: 1–2% (landing page not yet optimized)
Phase 1 target: >3% after landing page iteration in Week 2
Phase 2 target: >5% by optimizing ICP-specific landing pages
Weekly Content Review (30 min, Fridays)
Review the week's posts: Which hit above 1.5% engagement? What was the hook structure? Which pillar performed best? Identify one winner to repurpose as a carousel or video next week. Flag any post under 0.8% for hook analysis — don't just move on, understand why it missed. One insight from a failed post is worth more than one more generic win.
