ICP Framework

We're not selling to everyone who uses AI. We're selling to one person: the Head of Marketing at a 50-person company who needs results today.

Who We Sell To

The Primary ICP

The Marketing Head
Primary ICP — Marketing Lane
Role Head of Marketing / VP Marketing / Marketing Director
Company Size 30–120 employees (sweet spot: 50)
Industry B2B SaaS, professional services, e-commerce, digital agencies, fintech startups
Current Tech Stack
  • HubSpot or Salesforce (CRM)

Core Pains

This ICP is not chosen arbitrarily. The Head of Marketing at a 50-person company is the highest-leverage early adopter for Globster because they are simultaneously under-resourced and results-accountable. They have enough context to understand what a good AI agent would do for them — and enough daily pain to feel the gap immediately.

At 50 people, the company is too small for a dedicated marketing operations hire, too big to operate on gut instinct alone. The marketing head is expected to run campaigns, manage a content calendar, own the pipeline number, and justify headcount requests — all with a lean team of 2–3. They've tried generic AI tools and found them useful but unreliable. They are primed for something that just knows their job.

Critically, this person is also a natural amplifier. When they find a tool that genuinely saves them 5 hours a week, they share it. With their CEO. With peers at other companies. On LinkedIn. They are the node through which word-of-mouth propagates — which is why the bottom-up motion starts here.

Buying authority

This person can typically approve $0–$500/mo independently. Above that, they need sign-off from CEO or CFO. Our pricing must stay below the approval threshold during the individual activation phase, then expand post-proof.

The Psychology

Psychographic Profile

Features don't sell. Feelings do. Understand the emotional landscape of this buyer before you write a single word of copy.

Motivations
What drives them to act
  • Desire to be the person who brought AI into the company "the right way"
  • Ambition to grow without headcount — doing more with less is a badge of honor
  • FOMO: their peers are posting LinkedIn wins about AI-driven growth
  • Pride in craft — they want to produce great marketing, not just more marketing
  • Proving their strategic value to the C-suite beyond execution
Pain Points
What keeps them up at night
  • Every AI tool they've tried required hours of setup to get mediocre output
  • The pipeline is behind and the CEO is asking questions
  • Content backlog is months deep and growing
  • Can't prove that the AI tools they're paying for are actually generating ROI
  • Team burnout from too many tools, too many logins, too little clarity
Buying Triggers
What makes them pull the trigger
  • A peer in a Slack community posts a specific result ("saved 8h/week on reports")
  • They see a product demo that shows their exact workflow, not a generic use case
  • A painful Monday morning — missed deadline, bad campaign result, team conflict
  • CEO asks "what are we doing with AI?" — they need an answer fast
  • Free trial with zero setup friction — they can try it without a meeting

Scoring Framework

Qualification Criteria

Not every company with 50 employees is our ICP. Use these signals to qualify fast and disqualify faster.

✅ Qualification Signals
  1. 30–120 headcount with lean marketing team (1–3 people). The pain is structural, not situational. They are chronically under-resourced.
  2. Active on Slack or WhatsApp for business operations. Native channel delivery only works for people who actually use those channels for work. Must be present.
  3. Currently paying for at least one AI tool (ChatGPT, Jasper, Notion AI, etc.). They've crossed the AI adoption threshold. We're competing for consolidation, not evangelizing AI from scratch.
  4. Marketing owns pipeline or lead-gen responsibility. If marketing is purely brand/comms with no demand gen mandate, the ROI story changes.
  5. Individual contributor with autonomous purchase authority to $500/mo. No 6-month procurement cycles. They can say yes without a steering committee.
  6. Recent trigger event: growth round, hiring push, new leadership, product launch. Companies in motion are more likely to invest in tools that accelerate that motion.
❌ Disqualification Criteria — Who is NOT Our ICP
  • Enterprise with a dedicated marketing ops function. They already have people doing what our agent does. We're competing against headcount, not pain.
  • Fewer than 10 employees. At this size, the founder is doing marketing. They usually don't have established workflows for an agent to slot into.
  • Industries with strict AI-use compliance requirements (healthcare, legal, finance). Until we have compliance certifications, avoid regulated verticals in early GTM.
  • Companies with no Slack or WhatsApp presence. Our delivery model depends on these channels. Email-only teams miss 70% of our UX.
  • Buyer who needs IT approval for any SaaS tool. Our motion is bottom-up, self-serve. A 6-week procurement process kills the viral loop before it starts.
  • Primary marketing channel is offline or event-led only. Our Marketing Kit is optimized for digital channels. Offline-first businesses won't extract full value.

The duplication strategy
Each new lane is not a full product build — it's a new kit deployed on top of the same infrastructure, agent runtime, and delivery channels. The marketing lane proves the model: the onboarding flow, the pricing, the bottom-up viral loop, the activation metrics. Every subsequent lane inherits this proven architecture and adds only the role-specific prompt layer and integrations. This is why our expansion timeline is aggressive.
