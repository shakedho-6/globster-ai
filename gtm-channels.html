Section 03

The Viral Loop

The most efficient GTM motion isn't paid — it's a product that earns its own distribution. We design the Globster user journey to naturally pull teammates and peers into the product.

The Bottom-Up Flywheel

  1. Individual discovery — A head of marketing finds a LinkedIn post, a Reddit comment, or a referral link. Curiosity: "Can this actually do what it says?" Signs up solo.
  2. First value moment — Within 10 minutes they have a running agent that completed a real task (wrote a content brief, summarized competitor content, drafted a campaign). The "whoa" moment hits.
  3. Internal share — They Slack their team: "You have to try this." Globster makes this easy with a 1-click team invite and pre-written message copy in the app.
  4. Team adoption — Manager or team lead creates a team workspace. Agents are shared and customized for team workflows. WAA climbs because value is now collaborative.
  5. External referral — Team member attends a marketing meetup, posts on LinkedIn, or mentions Globster in a Slack community. New individuals find it. Loop repeats.

How We Design For This

  • In-product "share this agent" button appears immediately after first task completion — when delight is highest.
  • Team invite flow requires only a name + email. No credit card, no new account setup friction.
  • WhatsApp and Slack agents produce outputs that are natively shareable — a summary or brief lands directly in the conversation.
  • "Made with Globster" attribution footer on all agent outputs (opt-out available, but opt-out rate expected to be low).
  • D7 nudge: "Your agent has run X tasks. Know someone who'd get value from this?" with a pre-filled referral message.
  • Case study request at D30 for active users — turns retention into distribution content.
Section 04

What We're NOT Doing

Strategy is as much about what you skip as what you pursue. These five channels are explicitly off the table for Phase 1 — not because they don't work, but because they'd dilute focus before we have PMF.

📢
Paid Ads at Scale
We don't yet know which message converts. Running broad paid before finding the right hook means burning budget to validate copy, not acquire customers. We retarget warm audiences only.
📰
PR & Press
Press coverage for an unproven product drives awareness without activation. Heads of marketing don't read TechCrunch to find tools — they ask peers. PR is a Phase 3 play after we have case studies to prove the story.
🤳
Influencer Marketing
Most AI tool influencers have audiences of aspiring entrepreneurs, not decision-making marketing leaders at real companies. The audience mismatch makes this a brand play, not a conversion play. We build our own audience instead.
📧
Cold Email at Scale
Mass cold email in 2026 has sub-1% open rates for outbound AI tools. It damages our domain reputation and signals desperation. Founder-led outreach at 25–30 highly personalized messages/week is the right version of this — not a blast campaign.
🔍
SEO Content Farm
SEO compounds over 6–12 months. In Phase 1 we need signal within 6 weeks. Publishing 50 keyword-stuffed posts now would consume engineering and writing resources without measurable impact until we're already in Phase 3. We'll invest in SEO when retention is proven.
