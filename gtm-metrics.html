Success Metrics

Metrics without context are noise. Here's what we track, why it matters, and what we do when a number is off.

Section 01

North Star & PMF Formula

One metric to rule the roadmap. Three criteria to declare product-market fit. Everything else is context.

North Star Metric
>60%
Weekly Active Agents (WAA)
Of all agents deployed by active users, what percentage ran at least one task in the last 7 days? A WAA below 60% means agents are being set up but not actually used — which is the most dangerous form of false activation. WAA above 60% is our signal that Globster has become a habit, not a novelty. Every product decision should move this number.

Why These Three Together
D30 Retention >25% proves the product earns repeat behavior over a full month — not just first-week curiosity. It's the most objective retention bar for a B2B SaaS tool with weekly use expectations.

NPS >50 (the Promoter zone) proves users feel strongly enough to actively recommend us — not just click a 5-star. NPS in the 30–50 range signals satisfaction but not advocacy. We need advocacy.

Organic referral >20% proves word-of-mouth is actually happening in the wild — not just in response to a nudge email. When one in five new signups arrives without us doing anything, the flywheel is turning on its own.

Section 03

Leading vs. Lagging Indicators

Leading indicators tell you where you're going before you get there. Lagging indicators confirm you arrived. You need both — but you act on leading indicators.

Type Metric Definition What it predicts / confirms Check frequency
Leading Time to first task run How long from signup to the first completed agent task, in minutes. Predicts D7 retention. Users who run their first task in <10 min are 2–3× more likely to still be active at Day 7. Daily (per cohort)
Leading Kit deploy rate (Day 1) % of new signups who deploy at least one kit within 24 hours. Predicts activation. If <50%, onboarding has a friction point — diagnose immediately, don't wait for D7 data. Daily
Leading LinkedIn engagement rate % of post impressions that result in likes, comments, or shares. Predicts weekly signup volume 5–7 days later. A post with >2% engagement typically drives 15–30 signups the following week. Per post (immediate)
Leading Team invite sent rate % of activated users who click "invite team member" within 14 days. Predicts organic referral % and team-tier conversion. If invite rate drops, audit the in-product share flow — the prompt may have lost prominence. Weekly
Leading Tasks per agent (week 1) Average number of tasks run by a user's first agent in their first 7 days. Predicts D30 retention. Users who run 5+ tasks in Week 1 have 2× higher D30 retention than those who run 1–2 tasks. Weekly per cohort
Lagging D30 Retention % of users who signed up exactly 30 days ago who were active in the last 7 days. Confirms that activation turned into habitual use. PMF criterion. You can't act on this in time to save a cohort — use it to improve the next cohort's onboarding. Monthly (per cohort)
Lagging NPS Score Net Promoter Score from D30 survey. (% Promoters) − (% Detractors). Confirms product-market fit advocacy. A high NPS with low organic referral means users like the product but aren't active recommenders — close the gap with in-product share prompts. Monthly
Lagging CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost) Total marketing spend ÷ new paying customers in the same period. Confirms channel efficiency retroactively. Useful for budget allocation in next quarter — but by the time you see a high CAC, 4–6 weeks of budget have already been spent. Use leading channel metrics (engagement rate, CTR) to predict it earlier. Monthly
Lagging Organic referral % % of new signups in a period attributable to referral (friend, colleague, community word-of-mouth). Confirms the viral loop is working. Can't be influenced directly — it's the result of all the leading indicators (NPS, product value, team invite flow) working together. Weekly (but trends matter more than week-to-week)
Section 04

Review Cadence

Metrics only drive decisions if you look at them at the right time with the right questions. Here's exactly when we review what — and what we do when something's red.

Daily
Pulse Check
5 min, async
  • New signups in last 24h
  • Kit deploy rate (yesterday's signups)
  • Any support tickets opened
  • LinkedIn post engagement (if posted)
  • Any errors or infra alerts
Weekly
GTM Review
30 min, Fridays
  • Signups vs. target for the week
  • Activation rate for week's cohort
  • WAA across all active users
  • LinkedIn impressions + top post
  • Organic referral % this week
  • D7 data for cohort from 7 days ago
  • One red metric: root cause identified
Monthly
Cohort Analysis
60 min, first Monday
  • D30 retention for last cohort
  • NPS survey results + qualitative review
  • CAC by channel
  • Churn analysis: who left and why
  • Feature usage patterns: what % use each kit type
  • Decision: adjust onboarding, content, or channel mix?
Quarterly
Phase Gate
Half-day offsite
  • Phase go/no-go criteria assessment
  • ICP validation: is our customer evolving?
  • Channel mix review: what's working, what's not?
  • Pricing check: are we leaving money on the table?
  • Reset KPI targets for next phase
  • PMF criteria progress vs. projection

"If This Metric is Red" Response Guide

Metric Red = Below First Action (within 48h) Owner
Activation rate <50% Manually reach out to every non-activated user from the last 3 days. Offer a 10-min setup call. Identify where they dropped off in the onboarding flow. Founder
D7 Retention <30% Interview 5 churned users from the cohort within 48 hours. Ask one question: "What would have made you come back?" Do not build anything until you've done 5 interviews. Founder + Product
WAA <45% Check which agent types have the lowest task run rates. Are agents being deployed but sitting idle? Add an email trigger: "Your [agent name] hasn't run a task this week. Here's a quick prompt." Product
Signups / week <10 Don't touch paid. Review the last 5 LinkedIn posts: was engagement above 1%? If posts are performing but signups are low, the landing page is the problem. A/B test a new headline this week. Founder
LinkedIn engagement <0.8% The hook failed. Rewrite the top-performing old post from a different angle and re-publish. Do not write a new topic — test a different hook on a proven topic first. Founder
Organic referral % <10% Check if the in-product invite prompt is visible. Survey 10 active users: "Have you told anyone about Globster?" If yes but not tracked, fix attribution. If no, add a post-task share nudge. Product
